Ways we can help limit the effects of global warming!

Change a light

A compact fluorescent light bulb can save 150 pounds of carbon dioxide annually when one standard light bulb is replaced..

Drive less

Take public transportation, walk, bike, or carpool more frequently. For every mile you choose not to drive, you will save one pound of carbon dioxide!

Recycle more

Recycling just half of your household waste can result in an annual carbon dioxide savings of 2,400 pounds.

Check your tires

Maintaining appropriate tire pressure can increase your gas mileage by over 3%. Twenty pounds of carbon dioxide are kept out of the atmosphere for every gallon of fuel that is saved.

Use less hot water

Heating water uses a lot of energy. By taking shorter, colder showers and washing your clothing in cold or warm water rather than hot, you can reduce the amount of hot water you use (saving over 500 pounds of carbon dioxide annually).

Avoid products with a lot of packaging

You can save 1,200 pounds of carbon dioxide if you reduce your garbage by 10%.

Adjust your thermostat

It is possible to save almost 2,000 pounds of carbon dioxide annually by simply adjusting your thermostat by down 2 degrees in the winter and up 2 degrees in the summer.

Plant a tree

A single tree will absorb one ton of carbon dioxide over its lifetime.

Turn off electronic devices

Turning off your computer, audio, DVD player, and television while not in use will help you save thousands of pounds of carbon dioxide annually.
